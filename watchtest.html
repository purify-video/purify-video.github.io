What we CANNOT do

These would require injecting CSS into youtube-nocookie.com's iframe, which is blocked by the browser's same-origin policy. No amount of PHP proxying or postMessage gymnastics gets around this:

  • Hide the YouTube logo in the player chrome
  • Change the colour of the progress bar
  • Hide individual buttons (share, watch later, etc.) on the player
  • Change the player's font, button shape, or layout
  • Style the "Watch on YouTube" link on pause
  • Modify the related videos panel that appears on end

The only way to fully restyle the player is to extract the raw video stream and play it in your own HTML5 <video> element with a player like Plyr or Video.js.
